How to Find Work as a Freelance Journalist ...

Grind Time: Constant
Helpfulness rating: 95%

It can be tough for freelance journalists to find publications for their work. Here are just a few tips to get your career growing as an up and coming journalist.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Computer
  • Examples of Past Journalistic Work
  • Networking Skills
  • Open Mind
  • Good Attitude
  • Appreciation for the Freelance Grind

  1. Sign up on freelance writing websites.

    Try signing up to freelancing websites such as Freelancer, Elance or oDesk. These websites have small one-time assignments offered by people who need writers.

  2. Build a network.

    If you know of any other freelancers, ask them about what kind of opportunities exist. Talk to people in the industry who may know of any jobs or assignments available.

  3. Join an online community.

    Online journalist communities on LinkedIn or Facebook can help you get work as a freelance journalist. Start talking to other journalists to help build your network and gain more knowledge.

  4. Research publications that you can write for.

    Find different publications and get to know their writing styles/the kinds of topics they usually write. Try to find contact information for editors to send them pitches for articles.

  5. Build a website.

    A website can show off past examples of your work and give potential employers an idea of the type of content they can expect from you. Remember to include your contact details.

  6. Start pitching article ideas to editors.

    A pitch is a small summary of an idea that you have for an article. Typically freelance journalists pitch article ideas to editors who will either accept or reject it. Start writing multiple pitches to different editors and see if you get any responses.
